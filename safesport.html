Zero Tolerance

USAV & GEVA
SafeSport.

USA Volleyball and Regional Volleyball Associations have adopted zero tolerance for abuse and misconduct — on the court and off it. NYC Impact fully supports and enforces this standard.

Our Commitment

A Safe, Fun
Environment.

The safety of participants is of paramount importance. Together, USA Volleyball and the Regional Volleyball Associations provide training, educational materials, and resources for regions, clubs, coaches, and parents. The safety of minors lies with everyone involved in the sport — not the sole responsibility of any one person at the club, regional, or national level.

Misconduct of any kind can damage an athlete's psychological wellbeing, harm their competitive performance, and cause them to leave the sport entirely. NYC Impact is committed to fostering an environment where that never happens.

What Is Abuse & Misconduct?

Know The
Categories.

Child Sexual Abuse

Any sexual activity with a child where consent is not or cannot be given.

Bullying

A persistent, repeated pattern of behavior intended to cause fear, humiliation, or harm.

Hazing

Coercing or requiring humiliating, unwelcome, or dangerous activity as a condition of group membership.

Harassment

A repeated pattern of behavior meant to establish dominance or power over an individual or group.

Emotional Misconduct

Deliberate, non-contact behavior with the potential to cause emotional or psychological harm.

Physical Misconduct

Contact or non-contact behavior that can cause physical harm to an athlete or participant.

Sexual Misconduct

Any nonconsensual, forced, or coerced sexual interaction, including any interaction between an adult and a minor.

Maltreatment

The general term describing all forms of child abuse — physical, sexual, neglect, and emotional.

Definitions courtesy of the U.S. Center for SafeSport and USA Volleyball. Read the full USAV SafeSport handbook.

Training

SafeSport
Training.

All adults working directly with juniors — coaches, officials, club directors, and tournament directors — are required to complete SafeSport training through the USAV Academy. Visit our USA Volleyball Registration page for step-by-step instructions.

GEVA also maintains its own SafeSport resources, available at geva.org/safesport.

Need to Report a Concern?
Visit our Incident Report page for how to report a safety concern to NYC Impact, USA Volleyball, or the U.S. Center for SafeSport.
